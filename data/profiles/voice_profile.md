# Voice Profile

## Overall Style
- Direct and conversational
- Sounds like a real person, not polished corporate writing
- Avoids sounding like AI wrote it
- No fluff or filler
- No em dashes
- Short to medium sentences
- Slightly informal but still professional

## Tone
- Confident but not over-claiming
- Honest about experience and gaps
- Practical, grounded in real work
- Not overly enthusiastic or salesy

## Writing Preferences
- Avoid buzzwords unless they are actually relevant
- Prefer simple language over complex phrasing
- Do not stack adjectives
- Do not exaggerate impact
- Keep things defensible if asked in an interview

## What to Avoid
- "I am passionate about…"
- "I am excited to apply…"
- Overly polished or generic intros
- Claims that can’t be backed up with real examples

## Good Example Tone
"I’ve spent most of my time working between product, engineering, and customers, especially around integrations and distributed systems. A lot of my work has been figuring out how things actually behave in production and helping teams make that reliable."

## Bad Example Tone
"I am a highly motivated and results-driven professional with a proven track record of delivering innovative solutions."

## Cover Letter Guidance
- Keep it short
- Get to the point quickly
- Reference something specific about the role if it’s obvious and real
- Do not force excitement
- End simply, no dramatic closing
